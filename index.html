My eye and the color within

Slaven Stevanovski

Born in the country that was created and destroyed in the 20th century, yet he still creates and destroys in the 21st century. With scattered origins "from Vardar to Triglav", he mostly lives in the Yugoslav capital and works and doesn't work wherever he can or can't. He is a master of unexpectedness and paradox, which he recognizes and invokes in wide social aura around him. He provokes and teases, so you better stay alert. As a modern alchemist and medicine man with an answer and a smile to every question, he mostly paints.

THE DISPOSITION OF A VISUAL PREJUDICE

The fields of rolling clouds have filled the space, perplexing with the hills of wavy oceans resting in the shade of colorful meadows in the skies high above them. Just like the beginning of a tale of Genesis of an inner Universe – a birth of the Universe of a Soul (or of THE Soul) seen by an outer eye, or maybe a Universe of an Eye seen by the outer Soul, the tale told on canvasses, a tale older than time, carefully leads us through delicate oscillating planes of neighboring worlds. With light being the common denominator, and the Light being a credo, the colorful tales on paintings of Slaven Stevanovski lead us to what we sometimes take for granted – the ever-lasting discourse on disillusionment and the illusionary; or senses vs. heavens; or spring breeze in a prairie vs. reflection on high cliffs of the mountains, bathed in light of two bright suns, on a lonely planet in the Proxima Centauri constellation, if you will... Stevanovski’s canvasses have an amazing ability to bring for a brief moment, a virtue of being the creator of his/her own reality, and his/her own surrounding, to a spectator, and they do this without spectators ever being guilty for this eternal process. There is an ancient word in Hungarian language (and one of the longest words in any of this world’s languages) with the meaning – you can never be desecrated for constantly behaving in the same manner. And just like this word “Megszentségteleníthetetlenségeskedése-
itekért” describes it – the paintings of Slaven Stevanovski relieve you of any guilt for being, or, even better, for not being the part of this overall Matrix, the existence of which has been discussed from the beginnings of the Time (or the beginnings of what we actually perceive as time - the way we perceive it).

George Diamond-Illusionist

Juicy "Cooked" Pictures of Slaven Stevanovski

On the day of the water fast, I got an invitation to write a letter about Slaven Stevanovski's paintings. Visualizing images of his work, possibly due to hypoglycemia caused by physical hunger, their appearances and procedures remind me of very tasty meals. Like a good cook, Slaven doesn't use too many colors, that is groceries. Neither an appealing picture nor a tasty dish is crammed with details. Selected or found ingredients need to be well prepared, connected in the finest order, with a hint of spice and served with care and love. When Amenhotep, building a cult of monotheism, asked Egyptian priests to name a single word that could represent the God, after weeks of considerations, they came up with the idea of MEASURE. And it's no surprise that ancient Greek civilization and philosophy revolve around measure. The measure is the secret to Slaven's paintings. Measure of format and density, measure of color and thickness, measure of tones and moves, measures of figures and dynamics. In painting, as well as in food preparation, there is an alchemical process that by transforming matter brings the energy of fulfillment through our senses, to the bodies when we speak about the food, and to the soul, with a lot of taste and style, perceptive juiciness and emotional nutritiveness – that is how paintings of Slaven Stevanovski feed us using measure and sensitivity.

DoA Ivan Pravdić
